How CoolSculpting Works and What You Can Expect

CoolSculpting uses a method called cryolipolysis. In simple terms, this means freezing fat cells to the point where your body naturally processes and eliminates them over time. Unlike liposuction, there are no incisions, needles, or anesthesia involved.

During your treatment, we place an applicator on the target area, and the CoolSculpting machine begins to lower the temperature to freeze the fat beneath the skin. The process usually takes between 35 and 60 minutes per area. Most of our clients use the time to catch up on emails, watch a show, or relax with music.

After the session, you may experience some redness, slight swelling, or numbness in the treated area, but these effects are temporary. There’s no need to take time off work, and many people head straight back to their daily routines.

Over the following weeks, your body gradually processes the dead fat cells. You’ll start seeing changes around three weeks after treatment, with full results typically visible by the third month.

Questions We Hear Often

Does CoolSculpting hurt?

Most people describe the feeling as cold and slightly tingly for the first few minutes, but that sensation quickly fades. After the session, some experience numbness or mild tenderness in the treated area, but it doesn’t interfere with normal activities.

How many treatments will I need?

It depends on your goals and the area being treated. Some people are happy with one session, while others benefit from two or more. We’ll go over all of this during your consultation.

Is CoolSculpting permanent?

Yes, the fat cells destroyed by CoolSculpting are gone for good. However, maintaining results depends on keeping a healthy lifestyle. If you gain weight, new fat cells can form.
